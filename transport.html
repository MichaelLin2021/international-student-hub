Bus Travel Around UEA

Local buses are one of the easiest ways to travel between UEA and Norwich city centre. The Blue Line routes are especially useful for students.

Blue Line

Bus 25

Travels between Norwich city / railway station and UEA. Useful for daily travel between campus and the city.

Blue Line

Bus 26

Connects UEA with the city centre and railway station. Useful for shopping, social events and rail connections.

Blue Line

Bus 26A

Another Blue Line service connected with UEA routes. Students should check live times before travelling.

UEA states that Blue Line buses 25, 26 and 26A run between Norwich railway station, city centre and campus. Journey time is around 30 minutes depending on traffic.

Travelling by Train

Norwich railway station connects students to many UK destinations. Greater Anglia operates services between Norwich and London Liverpool Street.

Popular Route

Norwich → London Liverpool Street

Useful for visiting London, travelling to airports, or connecting to other UK cities.

Tip

Book in Advance

Advance tickets can be cheaper. Greater Anglia lists advance fares from £12 one-way when booked directly.

Student Saving

Railcard

Students can consider a 16–25 Railcard or other eligible railcards to reduce train costs.

Cycling Around UEA

Cycling can be a flexible and low-cost option for students living near campus. Students should use lights at night and a secure lock.

  • Useful for students living close to campus.
  • Can reduce transport costs.
  • Always use bike lights and secure cycle parking.
  • Check safe routes before cycling at night.

Quick Recommendations

  • UEA to city centre: Use Blue Line buses 25, 26 or 26A.
  • UEA to railway station: Use Blue Line bus services or check live routes in the First Bus app.
  • Regular commuting: Consider bus passes or cycling.
  • Travelling to London: Use Greater Anglia from Norwich to London Liverpool Street.
  • Before travelling: Always check live bus or train times.
