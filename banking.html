Bank Account Setup

Step-by-step banking guidance for international students in Norwich.

Why Open a UK Bank Account?

A UK bank account can make everyday student life easier. It can help with paying rent, receiving refunds, using contactless payments, managing subscriptions, and avoiding some international card fees.

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Compare different banks and account types.
  2. Prepare your documents.
  3. Apply online or book an appointment at a branch.
  4. Wait for approval and identity checks.
  5. Activate online banking and your debit card.

Documents You May Need

  • Passport or national ID card.
  • Student ID or university enrolment confirmation.
  • Proof of address in the UK.
  • Visa or immigration status information if requested.

Requirements can vary between banks, so students should always check the bank’s own website before applying.

Possible Bank Options in Norwich

UEA does not officially recommend one specific bank. The options below are examples of banks students commonly compare when choosing a UK account.

HSBC

HSBC has a Norwich branch on London Street. This may be useful for students who prefer a bank with international experience and in-person branch access.

  • Possible benefit: familiar international banking brand.
  • Good for: students who want online banking plus branch access.

Barclays

Barclays has banking locations in Norwich, including records showing a Barclays presence at the University of East Anglia Banking Hall, University Plain, NR4 7AF.

  • Possible benefit: convenient if campus-based services are available.
  • Good for: students who prefer a widely used UK bank.

Lloyds Bank

Lloyds lists banking locations in Norwich and provides online banking services. It may be useful for students who want a traditional high street bank.

  • Possible benefit: UK-wide banking network.
  • Good for: students who want both online and branch services.

NatWest

NatWest provides a branch and service locator that students can use to find nearby branches, banking hubs or ATMs.

  • Possible benefit: easy online branch/service search.
  • Good for: students who want to compare nearby services before visiting.

Choosing a Bank: Quick Checklist

  • Can you apply online?
  • Does the bank accept your documents?
  • Is there a branch or ATM near campus or your accommodation?
  • Is the mobile banking app easy to use?
  • Are there any account fees or international transfer charges?
