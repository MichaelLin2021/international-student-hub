Money setup

Bank Account Setup

Practical steps and document checks for international students preparing to open a UK bank account.

Process

Step-by-Step Banking Plan

Step 1

Compare banks

Check online application options, branch access, app reviews, fees and document rules.

Step 2

Prepare documents

Gather identity, student status and UK address evidence before applying.

Step 3

Apply and verify identity

Apply online or book a branch appointment, then wait for identity checks and account approval.

Step 4

Activate safely

Set up online banking, keep security details private and activate your debit card.

Bank options

Examples to Compare

UEA does not recommend one specific bank. These examples help students understand what to compare.

Branch access

HSBC

Useful for students who want a familiar international banking brand with online banking and branch support.

High street bank

Barclays

A widely used UK bank option. Students should check current local services and application requirements.

UK network

Lloyds Bank

May suit students who want a traditional bank with online services and a UK-wide presence.

Locator tools

NatWest

Provides online tools for finding branches, banking hubs and ATMs before visiting.

Digital banks

App-based options

Some students compare digital banks for quick setup, but document and eligibility requirements still matter.

Safety

Avoid scams

Never share banking passwords, one-time codes or card details with anyone claiming to "help" open an account.

Choosing well

Quick Questions

  • Can you apply online with your documents?
  • Is there a branch, ATM or banking hub near campus or your accommodation?
  • Are there monthly fees, transfer fees or international card charges?
  • Does the mobile app work well for everyday student life?

Important note

This prototype provides general information only. Students should check official bank websites and UEA guidance before making financial decisions.
