Quick answers

Frequently Asked Questions

Short answers to common questions about arrival, accommodation, healthcare, transport and everyday support.

Before arrival

Arrival and Visa

Do I need a visa?

If you do not already have immigration permission for the UK, you will normally need the correct student immigration route before UEA can formally register you. Check official UEA and UK government guidance.

Where can I get visa support?

UEA has International Student Advisers within the Student Life Team. Students can contact them for visa and international student support.

What should I keep with me when travelling?

Keep your passport, visa information, accommodation confirmation, university details and emergency contacts accessible during travel.

Healthcare

GP and Emergency Help

How do I register with a GP?

New students can register with a GP through the UEA Medical Centre website or another local GP practice. GP registration is important for non-emergency healthcare.

What should I do in an emergency?

Call 999 for emergencies. Use NHS 111 for urgent medical advice that is not life-threatening.

Should I wait until I am ill to register?

No. Registering early makes it easier to access care if you need support later.

Living in Norwich

Accommodation and Transport

Should I live on campus or in the city?

On-campus accommodation is convenient for classes and university support. City accommodation may suit students who prefer shops, restaurants and social spaces nearby.

What should I check before signing a contract?

Check rent, contract length, deposit, bills, location, travel time and cancellation rules.

How do I get from UEA to Norwich city centre?

Students commonly use local buses between UEA and Norwich city centre. Check route information and live times before travelling.

Student life

Support and Community

Where should I ask for help?

Use UEA Student Services, your academic adviser, the Students' Union or trusted university staff. For international student matters, contact the international support team.

How can I meet people?

Join societies, attend welcome events, join course groups and take part in international student activities.

What if I feel overwhelmed?

Speak to Student Services or your academic adviser. Asking early is normal and can prevent small problems becoming harder to manage.
