FAQ

Common questions from international students

Before Arrival

Do I need a visa?

If you do not already have immigration permission for the UK, you will normally need the correct student immigration route before UEA can register you formally.

Where can I get visa support?

UEA has International Student Advisers within the Student Life Team. Students can contact them for visa and international student support.

Healthcare

How do I register with a GP?

New students can register with a GP through the UEA Medical Centre website. GP registration is important for accessing non-emergency healthcare in the UK.

What should I do in an emergency?

Call 999 for emergencies. Use NHS 111 for urgent medical advice that is not life-threatening.

Accommodation

Should I live on campus or in the city?

On-campus accommodation is convenient for classes and university support. City accommodation may be better for students who prefer shops, restaurants and social spaces nearby.

What should I check before signing a contract?

Check rent, contract length, deposit, bills, location and cancellation rules.

Transport

How do I get from UEA to Norwich city centre?

Students commonly use local buses between UEA and Norwich city centre. Check live times and tickets before travelling.

Is cycling possible?

Yes. UEA provides information about cycling routes and campus cycle storage.

Daily Life

Where should I ask for help?

Use UEA Student Services, your academic adviser, the Students’ Union, or trusted university staff. For international student matters, contact the international student support team.

How can I meet people?

Join societies, attend welcome events, join course groups and take part in international student activities.
