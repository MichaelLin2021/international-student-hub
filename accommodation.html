Official UEA accommodation
On campus

UEA Residences

Good for students who want the simplest first-year setup, short walks to teaching spaces and direct access to campus support.

  • Bills are usually included.
  • Close to library, teaching and campus services.
  • Useful if this is your first time living in the UK.
On campus

Crome, Hickling and Barton Houses

Modern campus residences with en-suite room options and shared kitchen facilities.

  • Convenient for lectures and societies.
  • Resident Life support nearby.
  • No daily commuting cost.
City centre

Crown Place

Private student accommodation in Norwich city centre, suitable for students who want strong social spaces and quick city access.

  • Studios, en-suite rooms and shared flats.
  • Gym, cinema, games area and study spaces.
  • Approx. 25 minutes to UEA by bus.

City centre

Pablo Fanque House

A modern private hall option with en-suite rooms, studios and shared facilities for students who prefer city living.

  • Study rooms, gym and social spaces.
  • Approx. 20 to 30 minutes to UEA by bus.
  • Useful for students who want private halls rather than campus halls.

Decision guide

How to Choose

First time in the UK On-campus accommodation can reduce uncertainty and commuting pressure. Best for support
Prefer city life Private halls may be closer to shops, restaurants and social venues. Best for independence
Lower budget Compare rent, bills, deposits, transport and contract length together. Best for planning

Before You Pay

  • Verify the provider and official website.
  • Check whether bills and internet are included.
  • Read cancellation rules and contract length.
  • Never transfer money to an unverified person or account.
