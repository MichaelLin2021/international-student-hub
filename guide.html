New Student Guide

Step-by-step guide for international students settling into life at UEA.

Step 1: Complete University Registration

  1. Check your UEA email regularly before and after arrival.
  2. Complete online enrolment through the UEA student system.
  3. Collect your student ID card when instructed by the university.
  4. Save key university contacts and support pages for later use.

Step 2: Arrange Accommodation

  1. Check whether you are eligible for UEA accommodation.
  2. Compare on-campus accommodation and private student housing in Norwich.
  3. Check contract length, rent, deposit, bills and travel distance.
  4. Confirm your move-in date before travelling to Norwich.

UEA states that international students are usually guaranteed accommodation unless they have studied at UEA before. UEA accommodation also usually includes bills such as internet, gas and electricity.

Step 3: Open a UK Bank Account

  1. Choose a bank, such as HSBC, Barclays, Lloyds, NatWest or Santander.
  2. Prepare your passport, student ID, proof of address and proof of student status.
  3. Book an appointment online or visit a branch.
  4. Activate your account and set up online banking.

Step 4: Register with a GP

  1. Check whether you can register with the UEA Medical Centre or another GP near your address.
  2. Complete the GP registration form online.
  3. Use your GP for non-emergency healthcare and general medical advice.
  4. For emergencies, call 999. For urgent non-emergency medical advice, use NHS 111.

UEA explains that new students can register with a GP through the UEA Medical Centre website, and the process is quick and does not require downloading forms.

Step 5: Set Up Transport

  1. Check bus routes between UEA, Norwich city centre and Norwich railway station.
  2. Consider student bus tickets or passes if you travel often.
  3. Use cycling routes if you prefer travelling by bike.
  4. Plan late-night journeys safely, especially after social events.

UEA states that buses run frequently between campus, the city and the railway station. UEA also provides information about cycling and student travel options.

Step 6: Join Events and Build Support Networks

  1. Check student events, societies and welcome activities.
  2. Join course groups and international student communities.
  3. Ask experienced students for practical advice.
  4. Use university support services if you feel isolated or unsure.
